Verb Tense Chart

Past Present Future
Past Simple
  • An action completed at a specific time in the past or in a series
  • An action completed in the past where time is not mentioned

Examples: I worked all day yesterday, He tripped, fell and broke his arm

 Present Simple
  • Habits and routines
  • Things that are true in general, facts, permanent situations, states

Examples: I go to the cinema every Saturday, I live in Florence

 Future Simple
  • “Will” for spontaneous decisions, predictions, future uncertainties
  • “Going to” form for future intentions or near certainties

Examples: You’ll love this book!, I’m tired – I’ll probably just stay at home
Past Perfect
  • An action completed in the past before another past action
  • To express a reason for a past state

Examples: They got to the party at 10:00, but she had already left

 Present Perfect
  • An action that started in the past and continues to the present
  • An action that happened in the past and affects the present

Examples: He has lived in Florence since 1997, Where have I put my keys?

 Future Perfect
  • An action that will have been completed by a specific time in the future

Examples: Next July, my parents will have been married thirty years
Past Continuous
  • An action in progress at a specific time in the past
  • Interrupted or stopped by another past action

Examples: What were you doing last night at eight?, They were sleeping when the phone rang

 Present Continuous
  • An action in progress at the time of speaking
  • Trends or currently developing situations

Examples: She can’t come to the phone – she’s sleeping, I’m listening to a lot of Miles Davis

 Future Continuous
  • An action in progress around a point in the future
  • To predict the future – what may be happening

Examples: Don’t call me at eight - I’ll be watching Eastenders
Past Perfect Continuous
  • Emphasises the continuous or repeated nature of an action completed in the past before another past action

Examples: She hadn’t been paying attention when the teacher asked her a question

 Present Perfect Continuous
  • Emphasises the continuous or repeated nature of an action completed in the past and continues to the present

Examples: I’m tired – I’ve been studying all afternoon

 Future Perfect Continuous
  • To describe how long something will have been happening by a certain time in the future

Examples: By next Autumn, I will have been working here for twenty years
